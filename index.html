Our Story

LUMIÈRE was founded in 2019 in the heart of Kerala, India, where the air itself tells stories of jasmine, sandalwood, and monsoon rain. Our founder, inspired by generations of botanical knowledge, set out to create fragrances that capture the essence of Indian nature.

Today, LUMIÈRE stands at the intersection of traditional Indian perfumery and modern luxury, creating scents that resonate with the soul.

The Art of Applying Perfume: A Complete Guide

By Meera Krishnan | December 15, 2024 | 5 min read

Perfume is more than a finishing touch. It is an invisible extension of your personality, a way to leave a lasting impression long after you have left the room. But applying perfume correctly can make the difference between a scent that fades in an hour and one that enchants all day.

The Pulse Points

The warmest areas of your body are where perfume truly comes alive. These pulse points include the wrists, behind the ears, the base of the throat, the inside of your elbows, and behind the knees. The heat from these areas helps diffuse and amplify the fragrance.

The Distance Rule

Hold the bottle 15-20 cm from your skin when spraying. This allows the fragrance to distribute evenly rather than concentrating in one spot. A light mist that settles on the skin creates a much more natural and elegant scent cloud.

Layering for Longevity

For fragrances that last from morning to evening, layer with complementary products. Start with an unscented moisturizer (hydrated skin holds fragrance longer), then apply your perfume. Some of our clients even spray a light mist on their hairbrush before brushing, creating a subtle scent trail.

The Golden Rule

Never rub your wrists together after applying perfume. This friction breaks down the top notes and alters the fragrance composition. Instead, simply let it dry naturally and allow the scent to develop on your skin.
